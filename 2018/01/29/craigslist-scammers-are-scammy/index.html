CRAIGSLIST SCAMMERS ARE SCAMMY

For the first time ever, I included a phone number with my Craigslist ad. Oops.

My ad was selling old photo gear, i had put about 8 items together on one page. You could buy them all separately, or together for $750.

Here’s the first text i got:

I was psyched! Somebody wants to take everything at once, and pay even more than it will cost to ship? Fantastic!

But the more i texted with them, the less confident i became.

Hold on, someone from an Ohio area code is looking at Craigslist ads around Portland? They want to pay above asking price? They have no questions about individual items, and they want every single weird thing i advertised? In fact they haven’t even addressed the fact that the ad contains a bunch of items in it (do you have ‘it’ for sale)?

No. Hell, no. This is too weird. I needed the money very badly, but i had to resign myself to the fact it was a scam.

That night i received several more texts of the exact same kind. I responded to them the next morning, just to see how formulaic the chat would be. I’m dumping those in below:

Common elements:

  • There are no specific questions about your product, only generic ones, like “Do you have it” and “Is it in the original box”
  • They don’t haggle over price.  In fact, money doesn’t seem to be a problem.
  • They actually express some skepticism about you! “I hope this is not a scam”, “Hope you’re honest about the condition”
  • They want to ship it to someone other than themselves
  • In my case, the area codes were all WAY out of state.  The phone numbers in those screenshots are real, btw.

It seems like they have some software that scrapes craigslist for ads that contain a phone number.  Then the program sends a first text thats just the posting title and a generic question.  Its probably trolling thousands of people all the time, every day.

Needless to say, I took my phone number off the ad right quick.
